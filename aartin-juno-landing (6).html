The Story Behind Juno

Ancient Indian craft, made for modern living

The Juno base is turned on a foot-powered lathe from a single block of mango wood — a dense, beautifully grained hardwood grown and harvested sustainably by the same family in Rajasthan for three generations.

The shade starts as undyed cotton thread. Craftspeople resist-dye the threads before they are put on the loom, an ancient technique called ikat, so the pattern is woven in rather than printed on. Each shade is then hand-gathered into soft pleats and lined with pure silk, giving you that warm, honeyed glow no factory-made lamp can replicate.
